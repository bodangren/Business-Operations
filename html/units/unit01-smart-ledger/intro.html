üìö Essential Terms

Before we dive into Sarah's data, let's define the key terms you'll use throughout this unit:

Double-Entry Bookkeeping

A system where every transaction affects at least two accounts, and total debits must equal total credits. This creates automatic error-checking that catches mistakes before they become problems.

Example: When TechStart buys a $500 laptop, they debit Equipment (+$500) and credit Cash (-$500). The transaction balances.

Chart of Accounts

An organized list of all accounts used by the business, typically numbered for easy reference and logical organization (1000s = Assets, 2000s = Liabilities, etc.).

Example: 1000 = Cash, 1100 = Equipment, 2000 = Credit Card Debt

Trial Balance

A report listing all account balances to verify that total debits equal total credits. If they don't balance, you know there's an error to find and fix.

Example: All debit balances = $50,000, All credit balances = $50,000 ‚úì

Smart Ledger

Our term for an Excel-based accounting system that automatically checks for errors, highlights problems, and provides real-time financial insights to business owners and investors.
