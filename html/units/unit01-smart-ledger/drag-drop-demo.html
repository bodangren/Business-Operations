Exercise 1: Account Categorization

Master the accounting equation by categorizing accounts into Assets, Liabilities, Equity, Revenue, and Expenses. This foundational skill is essential for all accounting work.

Instructions

Exercise 2: Journal Entry Builder

Practice creating journal entries by dragging accounts into the correct positions and entering the appropriate amounts. Watch the totals update in real-time to ensure your entry balances.

Remember the Rules

  • Every journal entry must have equal debits and credits
  • Assets and expenses increase with debits
  • Liabilities, equity, and revenue increase with credits
  • Think about what the business is receiving vs. giving up

Exercise 3: Trial Balance Organization

Organize accounts by their normal balance side (debit or credit) and verify that the trial balance is mathematically correct. This exercise helps you understand which accounts naturally have debit vs. credit balances.

Normal Balances

Debit Balances (Left Side)
Assets, Expenses, Dividends, Losses
Credit Balances (Right Side)
Liabilities, Equity, Revenue, Gains

Tips for Success

Memory Aids

  • DEAL: Debits increase Dividends, Expenses, Assets, Losses
  • CLIP: Credits increase Capital, Liabilities, Income, Profit
  • The Accounting Equation: Assets = Liabilities + Equity (always!)

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Confusing "debit" with "bad" and "credit" with "good" (they're just directions!)
  • Forgetting that the accounting equation must always balance
  • Mixing up normal balances (assets are normally debit, not credit)
  • Not thinking about the business purpose of each transaction
