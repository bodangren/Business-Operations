üìÇ Organizing TechStart's Accounts

Why Organization Matters

Investors want to see that Sarah understands exactly what her company owns, owes, and earns. Proper account organization shows professional financial management.

üí∞

ASSETS

What TechStart Owns

Cash: Bank accounts, petty cash
Equipment: Laptops, office furniture
Accounts Receivable: Money clients owe TechStart
Rule: Assets increase with debits, decrease with credits
üí≥

LIABILITIES

What TechStart Owes

Credit Cards: Business credit card balances
Accounts Payable: Bills TechStart needs to pay
Loans: Money borrowed from banks
Rule: Liabilities increase with credits, decrease with debits
üë•

EQUITY

Owners' Investment

Capital: Money Sarah & partners invested
Retained Earnings: Profits kept in the business
Revenue: Money earned from clients
Rule: Equity increases with credits, decrease with debits

üß† Easy Way to Remember

Think of a balance scale:

  • Assets (what you own) go on the left
  • Liabilities + Equity (how you got those assets) go on the right
  • The scale must always balance!

üîÑ Double-Entry: The Error-Catching System

Why Sarah Needs This

Remember Sarah's $2,000 missed payment? Double-entry bookkeeping would have caught that error immediately. Every transaction affects at least two accounts, and debits must equal credits. If they don't balance, you know something's wrong.
