Unit 1: Smart Ledger Launch

Complete 10-day lesson plan using backward design principles

Duration: 10 class periods (2 weeks) Grade Level: 12th Grade Applied Math Course: Math for Business Operations

Stage 1: Identify Desired Results

Essential Question

How can we design a self-auditing ledger that would convince a potential angel investor we keep "clean books" from day 1?

Enduring Understandings

Students will understand that:

  • Accurate financial records are the foundation of business credibility and investor confidence
  • The accounting equation (Assets = Liabilities + Equity) governs all business transactions
  • Self-auditing systems prevent errors and build stakeholder trust through automation
  • Professional presentation of financial data communicates business competence

Students will know...

Accounting Content

  • The accounting equation and its application to transactions
  • Debit and credit rules for different account types
  • Trial balance purpose and construction
  • Common business transaction types and recording methods
  • Error detection and correction techniques

Excel Technical Skills

  • Excel Tables structure and benefits
  • SUMIF function syntax and applications
  • Conditional formatting rules and visual indicators
  • Error-checking formula construction
  • Professional spreadsheet formatting standards

Students will be able to...

Accounting Skills

  • Apply the accounting equation to analyze business transactions
  • Record debits and credits accurately for common business activities
  • Generate and interpret a trial balance for error detection
  • Identify and correct common recording errors
  • Explain accounting processes to non-expert audiences

Excel Skills

  • Create Excel Tables with structured references for dynamic data
  • Use SUMIF functions to aggregate financial data by category
  • Implement conditional formatting for visual error detection
  • Build error-checking formulas for data validation
  • Present data professionally for business audiences

Stage 2: Determine Acceptable Evidence

Performance Task (Summative Assessment)

Investor Pitch + Live Demo (Day 10)

Scenario: Students present their self-auditing ledger to a panel of local finance professionals (PTA members with finance backgrounds, local CPAs, business owners) as if seeking angel investment.

Performance Requirements:
  • 4-minute pitch explaining the ledger's features and investor benefits
  • Live Excel demonstration showing error-checking features in action
  • Q&A response addressing panel questions about accuracy and reliability
  • Professional presentation using business-appropriate language and visuals
Authentic Context:

This mirrors real investor due diligence where financial systems and controls are scrutinized before investment decisions.

Milestone Assessments (Formative)

Milestone 1 (Day 3)

Prototype Ledger with 10 Transactions

  • All transactions correctly posted using debit/credit rules
  • Excel Table format with proper headers and structure
  • Basic SUMIF formulas calculating account totals
  • Trial balance showing mathematical accuracy

Milestone 2 (Day 6)

Integrated "Red Flag" Rules

  • Conditional formatting highlighting negative balances
  • Visual indicators for missing or incomplete entries
  • Automated alerts for debit/credit imbalances
  • Professional color scheme and formatting

Milestone 3 (Day 7)

Trial Balance Auto-Check

  • Formula validation: ABS(sum_debits - sum_credits) = 0
  • Green/red indicator for balance status
  • Error identification and correction guidance
  • 100% accuracy on provided test dataset

Rubric for Performance Task

Criteria Weight Exemplary (A) Proficient (B-C) Developing (D)
Accuracy 45% All transactions correctly posted; trial balance reconciles perfectly; error-checking catches all planted errors Minor errors (1-2); trial balance reconciles with guidance; error-checking catches most issues Multiple errors; trial balance doesn't reconcile; limited error detection
Functionality 25% All SUMIF formulas work perfectly; conditional formatting comprehensive; system handles edge cases SUMIF formulas mostly correct; basic conditional formatting working; handles normal cases Some formulas broken; limited formatting; system fragile

Stage 3: Plan Learning Experiences and Instruction

Learning Sequence Overview

Phase 1: Hook & Foundation (Days 1-3)

Build engagement and establish foundational knowledge

Phase 2: Skill Development (Days 4-7)

Guided practice and skill building with scaffolded support

Phase 3: Application & Assessment (Days 8-10)

Independent application and authentic performance demonstration

Day 1: Launch & Explore

Focus: Hook students with authentic business challenge and form collaborative teams

Duration: 50 minutes

Opening Hook (15 minutes)

Entry Event: Founder Q&A

Setup: Zoom call with local startup founder or recorded video interview

Focus Questions:

  • "What bookkeeping disasters have you seen in your business or others?"
  • "How do investors evaluate financial record-keeping during due diligence?"
  • "What would make you confident in a startup's financial controls?"

Student Task: Take notes on investor concerns and requirements

Challenge Introduction (20 minutes)

  • Reveal: Show three anonymized CSV transaction dumps from real businesses
  • Problem: "These businesses need investor-ready financial controls. How can we help?"
  • Essential Question: Introduce the driving question and discuss what "self-auditing" means
  • Success Criteria: Show rubric and final presentation requirements

Team Formation & Planning (15 minutes)

  • Team Creation: Form groups of 2-3 students with mixed skill levels
  • Role Assignment: Financial Modeler, Documentation Lead, Quality Auditor
  • Business Selection: Choose from provided startups (food truck, e-commerce, tutoring) or propose own
  • Initial Planning: Begin planning approach using provided handout

Materials Needed

  • Transaction CSV files (3 different business types)
  • Founder interview video or Zoom setup
  • "Choosing Your Venture" handout
  • Team roles description sheet
  • Project planning template

Day 2: Accounting Equation & Excel Tables

Focus: Build foundational accounting knowledge and introduce Excel Tables

Duration: 50 minutes

Warm-up Review (5 minutes)

  • Quick team check-in on business selection and roles
  • Preview today's learning objectives

Mini-Lesson: Accounting Equation (15 minutes)

Direct Instruction Focus

Core Concept: Assets = Liabilities + Equity

Teaching Method: Interactive demonstration using real business examples

  • Show how every transaction affects the equation
  • Demonstrate T-account structure and debit/credit rules
  • Use student business examples for relevance
  • Check for understanding with quick partner discussions

Excel Tables Demo (15 minutes)

  • Screen Share: Convert CSV data to Excel Table step-by-step
  • Benefits: Explain structured references and dynamic expansion
  • Best Practices: Proper headers, data types, formatting
  • Student Follow-Along: Students practice with their chosen business data

Guided Practice (15 minutes)

  • Students convert their CSV data to proper Excel Table format
  • Add appropriate headers for ledger columns
  • Teacher circulates providing individual support
  • Peer helpers assist struggling students

Materials Needed

  • Sample ledger template (Excel file)
  • Accounting equation visual aid
  • T-account practice sheets
  • Excel Tables quick reference guide

Day 3: Journal Entries Practice

Focus: Practice recording transactions and reach Milestone 1

Duration: 50 minutes

Guided Practice: Transaction Recording (25 minutes)

  • Demonstration: Record 3 sample transactions as whole class
  • Think-Aloud: Teacher models decision-making process
  • Partner Practice: Students record 5 transactions in pairs
  • Check & Discuss: Review answers and common mistakes

Independent Work: Milestone 1 (20 minutes)

Milestone 1 Assessment

Task: Record 10 transactions for chosen business

Success Criteria:

  • All debits and credits correctly applied
  • Excel Table format maintained
  • Trial balance calculation attempted
  • Basic formatting applied

Peer Review Protocol (5 minutes)

  • Exchange ledgers with another team
  • Use checklist to identify errors
  • Provide constructive feedback
  • Return work with suggestions

Materials Needed

  • Journal entry worksheet with 10 sample transactions
  • Peer review checklist
  • Trial balance calculation guide
  • Business transaction reference sheet

Assessment Strategies & Differentiation

Formative Assessment Strategies

  • Exit Tickets: Daily 2-3 question checks on key concepts
  • Peer Review: Structured feedback using specific checklists
  • Teacher Conferences: 5-minute check-ins with each team
  • Self-Assessment: Students rate their confidence and progress
  • Digital Portfolio: Students document learning journey

Feedback Strategies

  • Specific & Actionable: "Add SUMIF formula to cell C15 to total debits"
  • Growth-Oriented: Focus on progress and next steps
  • Timely: Feedback within 24 hours of milestone submission
  • Multiple Sources: Teacher, peer, and self-feedback

Differentiation Support

For Struggling Students

  • Scaffolded Formulas: Provide partial SUMIF syntax templates
  • Step-by-Step Guides: Visual walkthroughs for each Excel skill
  • Peer Tutoring: Pair with students strong in Excel
  • Alternative Assessment: Oral explanation option for presentations

For Advanced Students

  • Extension Challenges: Dynamic dropdowns for account selection
  • Peer Teaching: Support struggling classmates
  • Advanced Features: Explore pivot tables or basic VBA
  • Leadership Roles: Facilitate team discussions and planning

For English Language Learners

  • Vocabulary Support: Business terms glossary with visuals
  • Translation Tools: Key concepts in home language
  • Visual Aids: Infographics and flowcharts for processes
  • Collaborative Support: Mixed-language team structures
