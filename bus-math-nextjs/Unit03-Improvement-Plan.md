## Lesson Tasks

### Lesson 01
- Skill: `agents/skills/launch-lesson/SKILL.md`
- Focus: launch the investor question, "What story do these numbers tell?"
- Tasks:
  - [ ] Start with Sarah's interview video and a statement-confusion problem.
  - [ ] Introduce the unit scoreboard around profit, solvency, and cash.
  - [ ] Keep the lesson focused on need and storyline, not statement mechanics yet.
- Done when:
  - [ ] Students can explain why one statement alone is not enough.
  - [ ] The lesson previews the three-statement flow.

### Lesson 02
- Skill: `agents/skills/accounting-principles/SKILL.md`
- Focus: build the income statement from trial balance or journal data.
- Tasks:
  - [ ] Use clear grouping logic for revenue and expense lines.
  - [ ] Keep strong representational supports in Phases 2-3.
  - [ ] Use Phase 4 for repeated statement-building practice.
- Done when:
  - [ ] Students can prepare and interpret a basic income statement.
  - [ ] The close points forward to balance-sheet effects.

### Lesson 03
- Skill: `agents/skills/accounting-principles/SKILL.md`
- Focus: build the balance sheet and retained earnings logic.
- Tasks:
  - [ ] Keep the equation and statement tie-outs visible.
  - [ ] Use mini balance-sheet representations before fully abstract forms.
  - [ ] Include explanation of why retained earnings links the story across periods.
- Done when:
  - [ ] Students can tie assets, liabilities, and equity correctly.
  - [ ] Students can explain how income affects equity.

### Lesson 04
- Skill: `agents/skills/accounting-principles/SKILL.md`
- Focus: indirect cash flow statement and ratio/story interpretation.
- Tasks:
  - [ ] Keep this lesson non-Excel.
  - [ ] Teach how profit and cash differ before introducing workbook linking.
  - [ ] Use Phase 4 to practice building or interpreting the statement and ratios with minimal scaffolding.
- Done when:
  - [ ] Students can explain operating, investing, and financing flow at a basic level.
  - [ ] Students can connect the three statements conceptually.

### Lesson 05
- Skill: `agents/skills/excel-lessons/SKILL.md`
- Focus: build cross-sheet links and statement checks in Excel.
- Tasks:
  - [ ] Teach the workbook architecture directly.
  - [ ] Use a safe rehearsal for linking logic before the live workbook sprint.
  - [ ] Require explicit tie-out checks in the workbook.
- Done when:
  - [ ] Statements are linked correctly.
  - [ ] The workbook shows visible integrity checks.

### Lesson 06
- Skill: `agents/skills/excel-lessons/SKILL.md`
- Focus: build KPI dashboard and executive summary layer.
- Tasks:
  - [ ] Keep the dashboard tied to the underlying statements.
  - [ ] Require at least one visual plus one written takeaway.
  - [ ] Phase 5 must ask students to defend the financial story in plain language.
- Done when:
  - [ ] The workbook supports a credible executive-facing summary.
  - [ ] Students can cite ratios and statement evidence clearly.

### Lesson 07
