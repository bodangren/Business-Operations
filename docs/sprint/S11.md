## Problem Statement
Lesson 2 already teaches and practices fixed-vs-variable classification in depth. Repeating the same interaction in Lesson 3 reduces cognitive novelty and weakens progression.

Lesson 3 should begin where Lesson 2 ended: students already know cost structure basics and now need to make pricing decisions using contribution margin, break-even, and capacity constraints.

## Planned Learning Flow (Student Experience)
1. **Contribution Margin Sprint**
   - Students compare 3 realistic price points for the same service.
   - They compute contribution margin ($ and %) and identify tradeoffs.
2. **Break-Even Ladder**
   - Students use one fixed-cost baseline to calculate break-even units for each price.
   - They rank options from easiest to hardest to break even.
3. **Capacity Reality Check**
   - Students test each option against a realistic monthly project capacity.
   - They determine which strategy is mathematically profitable and operationally realistic.
4. **Target-Profit Reverse Solve**
   - Students work backward from a target monthly profit.
   - They solve for required price or required units, preparing for Goal Seek and data tables in later lessons.
